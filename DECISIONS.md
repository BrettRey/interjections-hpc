# DECISIONS.md -- English Interjections as HPC

2026-03-14 — Target journal: Journal of Historical Pragmatics (Benjamins). Chosen for fit with diachronic dimension (semantic bleaching paths, nay→yeah shift) and because JHP already publishes interjection work (Gehweiler 2008 on gee!). Brett already has multiple papers in ELL and JL.

2026-03-14 — The HPC angle on interjections: the property cluster (non-referential, non-inflecting, prosodically isolated, syntactically supplemental, emotively laden) is maintained by identifiable mechanisms (semantic bleaching, prosodic isolation reinforcing syntactic independence). The distinctive contribution is that interjections push HPC harder than "respectable" categories because they're widely denied or marginalised as a category. The projectibility question is unusual: inference is primarily pragmatic rather than truth-conditional.

2026-03-14 — Primary empirical base: Brett's Wikipedia "Good Article" on English interjections, which covers phonology, morphology, syntax, semantics, pragmatics, variation, and the history of the category in English grammars. Corpus data from GloWbE and COHA already present.
