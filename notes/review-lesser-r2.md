## 1. Round 1 Concerns Addressed?

**Fixed:**

- **The road map is gone.** The ten-line itinerary ("Section 2 introduces... Section 3 surveys... Section 4 identifies...") has been cut entirely. Good. The section headings do this work, as they always did.

- **Data before theory.** The restructuring puts the property cluster (Section 2) before HPC theory (Section 3). This is a real improvement. The reader now sees what interjections actually do before being told what theoretical framework explains it. The paper earns its theory by showing its evidence first.

- **Subscript definitions no longer appear twice.** They live in Section 3.3, and Section 5.2 uses them without re-explaining the notation from scratch. The duplication I flagged between the old Sections 2.3 and 5.2 is resolved.

- **Section recap openings are trimmed.** Section 5 still opens with "Section 2 showed... Section 4 identified..." (lines 645-649), but it's shorter than before and the third sentence ("This section asks a different question") does genuine work. I'd still cut the first two sentences, but this is no longer a structural problem -- it's a stylistic preference.

**Partially fixed:**

- **The "threefold contribution" sentence survives** (lines 60-65). It's been compressed from the old 90-word monster to about 50 words, and the parenthetical glosses inside each item are gone. It's no longer the worst sentence in the paper. But "The contribution is threefold" is still a salesman's phrase. You could delete lines 59-65 and lose nothing -- the paper demonstrates its contributions without announcing them.

- **The Traugott & Dasher material** (Section 4.1.3, lines 500-524) is still there, though it reads somewhat more purposefully now. It does terminological work (distinguishing grammaticalization from pragmaticalization from nonce coinage), but it still adds three technical terms (subjectification, pragmaticalization, degrammaticalization) in the space of a single paragraph. The payoff line -- "Three diachronically distinct processes converge on the same synchronic category" -- could stand without the apparatus.

- **The Slater paragraph** (lines 310-319) is still a full paragraph in the main text, not the footnote I recommended. It's earned its keep slightly more now that it's in Section 3 rather than upfront, but it remains dutiful.

**Not fixed:**

- **The conclusion still recapitulates too faithfully.** Lines 976-996 walk back through the argument in order: cluster, stabilisers, perturbation test, field-relative decomposition. The reader has just read all of this. The final two paragraphs (lines 998-1016, the JHP-specific payoff and untested predictions) do new work. The first twenty lines of the conclusion are summary for a reader who wasn't paying attention.

- **"Is explained by the fact that" appears twice in consecutive sentences** (Section 5.2, lines 749-752). Still padding. Still there.

## 2. Best Sentences

**"A category real enough for the definitive grammar but thin enough for the textbook to cut."** (line 43-44)
Still the best sentence in the paper. I praised it last time and it hasn't gotten worse.

**"The items change; the category doesn't."** (line 611)
Still perfect. Six words, a semicolon, and the dynamic stability argument entire.

**"A list of co-occurring properties can't explain this pattern. A cluster maintained by mechanisms can."** (lines 272-273)
This is new, or newly positioned, and it works. The parallel structure carries an argument. The second sentence earns its brevity by following a concrete example (the *damn* case) rather than arriving from nowhere.

## 3. Worst Sentences

**"The contribution is threefold: (a) interjections as a test case for HPC at the margins; (b) the diachronic mechanisms that sustain the cluster, extending Gehweiler (2008)'s single-case analysis to a general theoretical claim; and (c) a distinctive form of projectibility -- primarily pragmatic rather than truth-conditional -- that extends HPC theory to a new domain of inference."** (lines 60-65)
Improved from Round 1 but still a contribution-announcement sentence. The (a)/(b)/(c) structure reads like a grant application. The paper is better than this sentence.

**"The overlap is explained by the fact that some mechanisms (prosodic isolation, non-referentiality) contribute to multiple HPCs simultaneously. The non-overlap is explained by the fact that other mechanisms are field-specific."** (lines 749-752)
I flagged this exact construction last time. "Is explained by the fact that" twice in two sentences is bureaucratic prose. "The overlap reflects shared mechanisms; the divergence reflects field-specific ones" says the same thing in half the words.

**"This section surveys those properties, drawing primarily on the empirical base documented in Ameka (1992), Quirk et al. (1985), and Huddleston and Pullum (2002). For each property, two questions are asked: does it co-occur with the others, and does observing it in a novel form licence inference to the rest of the cluster?"** (lines 72-76)
A methodological announcement disguised as a paragraph. The reader can see the pattern after one subsection. The passive voice ("two questions are asked") makes it worse. Cut the whole thing and start Section 2 at "Interjections are prosodically isolated."

## 4. Remaining Cut List

The paper is tighter than in Round 1. I'd estimate the restructuring saved 15-20% of the fat. What remains:

1. **Lines 59-65** (the threefold-contribution sentence). Delete. The paper demonstrates its contributions. **Saves 6 lines.**

2. **Lines 70-76** (the Section 2 methodological preamble). Delete. The subsection structure makes the method transparent. **Saves 6 lines.**

3. **Lines 274-276** ("The next two sections develop the theoretical framework..."). A vestigial road-map sentence. Delete. **Saves 2 lines.**

4. **Lines 310-319** (the Slater paragraph). Compress to a footnote, as I said last time. **Saves 6 lines.**

5. **Lines 401-406** (the Section 4 opening recap: "Section 2 documented a cluster... Two questions remain..."). The section heading ("Stabilisers: recruitment and maintenance") already answers the questions. Start at "Multiple diachronic pathways." **Saves 5 lines.**

6. **Lines 500-524** (the Traugott & Dasher apparatus in Section 4.1.3). Keep lines 490-499 (the convergence point) and lines 516-524 (the three-process conclusion). Cut the Traugott technicalia in between. **Saves 12 lines.**

7. **Lines 645-649** (the Section 5 opening recap). Start at "A circular account of projectibility says..." **Saves 4 lines.**

8. **Lines 749-752** ("is explained by the fact that" x2). Tighten as suggested above. **Saves 3 lines.**

9. **Lines 976-996** (the conclusion's faithful recapitulation). Keep the question ("What does categorizing something as an interjection let you predict?") and the answer. Cut the walk-through of sections. **Saves 10 lines.**

**Total: ~54 lines (~5%).** Less aggressive than my Round 1 list, because the restructuring already did half the work.

## 5. One Sentence of Advice

The restructuring fixed the architecture; now kill the remaining announcements -- every sentence that tells the reader what the paper is about to do, just did, or contributes, because the paper is now good enough to speak for itself.
