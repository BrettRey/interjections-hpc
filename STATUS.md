# STATUS.md -- English Interjections as HPC

## Current State

**Phase:** Project setup / seed idea
**Last updated:** 2026-03-14

### Genesis

Brett is the primary author of the Wikipedia "Good Article" on English interjections, which surveys the category across phonology, morphology, syntax, semantics, pragmatics, and variation, with corpus data (GloWbE, COHA) and historical coverage (Bullokar 1586 through CGEL 2002). The paper will reframe this empirical landscape through HPC theory.

### Target

Journal of Historical Pragmatics (John Benjamins). Gehweiler (2008) on *gee!* already published there; the diachronic mechanisms (bleaching paths, category membership shifts over time) are central to the journal's scope.

### Core Idea

Interjections are a particularly revealing test case for HPC theory because they've been denied category status (Jespersen), dismissed as uninteresting (Huddleston & Pullum Student's), or conceded as "marginal and anomalous" (Quirk et al.). If HPC can account for interjections, it handles the hard cases. The key contributions:

1. **Property cluster identification:** Non-referential, non-inflecting, prosodically isolated, syntactically supplemental, emotively laden, freely coined via onomatopoeia.
2. **Mechanisms:** Semantic bleaching (noun→interjection paths), prosodic isolation reinforcing syntactic independence, pragmatic conventionalisation.
3. **Projectibility:** What does categorising something as an interjection let you infer? The answer is primarily pragmatic (prosodic behaviour, syntactic non-integration, emotive/interpersonal force) rather than truth-conditional. This is the paper's distinctive theoretical contribution.
4. **Diachronic dimension:** Bleaching paths as mechanism trajectories; the nay→yeah shift and regional variation (lah, yaar, haba) as evidence of the cluster's dynamic stability.
5. **Boundary disputes as HPC predictions:** The graded boundaries with nouns, verbs, adverbs, fillers, and routine formulae are exactly what HPC predicts, not a problem to be explained away.
